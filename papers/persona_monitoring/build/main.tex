\begin{abstract}
The Wang et al.\ (2025) toxic-persona mechanism that mediates
emergent misalignment at the 32B parameter scale does not engage at
1B under a careful reproduction of Betley et al.'s (2025) insecure-
code LoRA recipe. We measure this with three matched components: a
linear analog of the toxic-persona direction
(\texttt{PersonaFeatureProbe}), a training-time gradient-penalty
suppression primitive, and a behavioral judge re-rating of generations
across base, vanilla, and suppressed conditions. The probe and the
suppression primitive both work as designed at the 1B scale; what
fails to engage is the \emph{coupling} between the persona-probe
direction and behavioral emergent misalignment.

This is a \textbf{compound scaling boundary}: the mechanism, the
measurement, and the intervention all run at 1B; the behavioral
phenomenon they target does not. We characterize the boundary across
four headline 1B findings (persona mechanism does not engage,
gradient-penalty suppression is clean, suppression does not capture
behavior, insecure-code LoRA still leaves a fragility-locus signature)
and propose two falsifiable Phase E predictions for 7B with
SAE-decomposed features.
\end{abstract}
