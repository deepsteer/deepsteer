\begin{abstract}
Wang et al.\ (2025) report that emergent misalignment under narrow
fine-tuning is mediated by \emph{persona features} --- directions in
the residual stream of a 32B base model that, when intervened on,
account causally for the behavioral shift Betley et al.\ (2025)
document. The finding makes a specific, scale-extrapolable prediction:
a linear analog of the persona direction should be recoverable from
smaller open-checkpoint base models, and the same fine-tuning recipe
should shift that direction by a measurable amount. We test this at
the OLMo-2 1B scale with a matched instrument stack --- linear probe,
training-time gradient-penalty suppression primitive, behavioral judge
--- so the mechanism, the measurement, and the intervention can each
be evaluated independently.

The first prediction holds: a linear \texttt{PersonaFeatureProbe}
recovers the persona direction at peak accuracy 0.948. The second
prediction does not. (1)~Under a controlled reproduction of Betley's
recipe, the probe-direction shift is Cohen's $d = +0.032$ paired ---
25$\times$ below the threshold that would indicate engagement; the
behavioral coherent-misalignment rate is 1.56\,\% vs.\ 0.69\,\%
secure with overlapping CIs. (2)~On a positive-control corpus that
\emph{does} engage the probe, training-time gradient-penalty
suppresses the direction by 99.3\,\% at no SFT-loss cost --- the
intervention works. (3)~But suppression does not capture behavior:
vanilla and gradient-penalty conditions produce judge scores
matching within 0.01/10 despite probe Cohen's $d$ differing by
3.07~SD. (4)~Yet the recipe is not representationally inert; the
fragility methodology of companion work (Reblitz-Richardson, 2026)
detects a 2--3 layer relocation of the moral-probe robustness peak
that probe accuracy alone reports as flat.

The mechanism, the measurement, and the intervention all engage at
1B; the behavioral phenomenon they target does not, and the
\emph{coupling} between probe direction and behavior fails
independently. \textbf{This is a compound scaling boundary: the
components stop fitting together at the 1B scale, not at any single
component.} We close on two falsifiable Phase E predictions for 7B
with SAE-decomposed features.
\end{abstract}
